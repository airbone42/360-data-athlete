# Static stretching pre-exercise — dose-response, 30s sweet spot

**Created:** 2026-05-16

## TL;DR

1. **30s per muscle is the evidence-based sweet spot.** Static stretching ≤ 30s per muscle group has **no** significant performance loss (pooled estimate = -1.1%). At ≥ 60s, strength/power drops 4-5% acutely.
2. **Behm's meta-analysis 2012:** "Acute static stretching induced performance changes were small to moderate (-3.7%) ... possibly because of reduced muscle activation." But: below 60s this effect is clinically irrelevant.
3. **Practice recommendation:** Static stretches in the warm-up are fine at ≤ 30s per muscle; ideally followed by dynamic activation (5-10 min) — the activation also neutralises the marginal effects of the 30s stretch.
4. **In the framework:** training_paradigms.md "hold time 30s, max 30s, no 45s or 60s" is conservative-evidence-based. Source reference is persisted.

## Findings

**Behm & Chaouachi 2012 meta-analysis:** dose-response between stretch duration and performance loss:

| Stretch duration / muscle | Performance loss |
|---------------------------|------------------|
| < 30s | -1.1% (statistically irrelevant) |
| 30-45s | -1.9% (clinically not relevant) |
| 45-60s | -3.0% (marginal) |
| ≥ 60s | -4.6% (significant) |
| 90s+ in isolation | up to -4.8% strength loss |

**Key finding:** Static stretching as part of a complete warm-up (followed by dynamic activation) has a **net positive** effect — the stretch-induced temporary activation reduction is neutralised by the activation element.

> "The benefits of static stretching before exercise seem to outweigh the disadvantages when the stretching is incorporated into a full warm-up and when the duration is reasonable (less than 60 seconds per muscle group)."

**Frontiers 2019 (Kataura et al.):** "Acute Effects of Static Stretching on Muscle Strength and Power: An Attempt to Clarify Previous Caveats" — confirms: stretching is not inherently harmful, but dose- and context-dependent.

## Open questions / Caveats

1. **Dynamic stretching** is not covered here — uncontroversially performance-neutral or slightly positive in the literature. Recommendation: after the static 30s, add 3-5 min of dynamic activation (leg swings, A-skips, etc.).
2. **PNF stretching** (proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation) produces a similar performance effect to static; dose-response less well studied.
