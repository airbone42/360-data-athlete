# Plyometric progression level 1-3 — ground-contact volume + bilateral→unilateral

**Created:** 2026-05-16

## TL;DR

1. **Volume recommendations by training status** (ground contacts/session, NSCA + Markovic 2010 + coach consensus):
   - **Beginner (< 3 months consistent plyo experience):** 50-80 contacts, 2×/week
   - **Intermediate (3-6 months):** 80-120 contacts, 2-3×/week
   - **Advanced (> 6 months progressive plyo):** 100-140 contacts high-intensity (up to 200 low-intensity), 2-3×/week
2. **Progression axes** (Markovic 2010, Davies 2015):
   - Volume → intensity → complexity (reactive strength)
   - Bilateral → unilateral
   - Low box → high box → depth jumps
   - Slow SSC (stretch-shortening cycle) → fast SSC
3. **Our framework levels 1-3 (specialist-ninja.md / config.example/training_paradigms.md):**
   - Level 1 (beginner): 40-60 contacts, bilateral (Box Jumps, Squat Jumps, Broad Jumps)
   - Level 2 (intermediate): 30-40 contacts unilateral (Single-Leg Hops, Split-Squat Jumps)
   - Level 3 (expert): 20-30 contacts reactive (Drop Jumps, Bounding)
   → Volume is conservatively low compared with the literature, but the bilateral→unilateral→reactive logic is consistent with Markovic 2010.

## Findings

**Markovic & Mikulic 2010 (Sports Medicine Review):** foundation for plyo progression. Three adaptation phases:
1. **Neuromuscular learning phase** (weeks 1-3): technique acquisition, low volume, bilateral
2. **Morphological adaptation** (weeks 3-8): volume increase, transition to unilateral
3. **Reactive phase** (weeks 8+): short ground contact (< 200 ms), depth jumps, bounding

**Ground-contact depth is the sharpest classification:**
- **Slow SSC (> 250 ms):** Squat Jumps, Box Jumps, Broad Jumps — concentric-dominant
- **Fast SSC (< 250 ms):** Drop Jumps, hopping, bounding — eccentric-concentric coupling, reflex-driven

→ The reactive phase (level 3 in our framework) is only sensible once neuromuscular and morphological adaptation are consolidated. "Drop jumps before 8 weeks of progressive plyo build-up" is contraindicated (tendon-overload risk).

**Unilateral transition as a quality marker:**
Markovic 2010: unilateral plyometrics replicate the run-specific SSC pattern (every step is single-leg). For runners, the bilateral → unilateral transition is the central specificity gain — so it should not be skipped.

## Open questions / Caveats

1. **Athlete-specific volume ceiling** is conservative relative to the literature — sensible with an Achilles history, but should be recalibrated against the general baseline regularly (every 8 weeks).
2. **Classification "reactive" (level 3)** should not be applied to every athlete. Only after 8+ weeks of stable level-2 sessions + without tendon symptoms.
