# Hamstring pre-lift static stretch — 2×30s before RDL variants

**Created:** 2026-05-16

## TL;DR

1. **2×30s static hamstring before RDL is evidence-based safe and useful.** Study consensus: ≤30s per muscle has no significant performance loss; with contracted hamstrings even a slight positive effect through improved ROM.
2. **Physiological logic:** shortened hamstrings limit hip-flexion ROM at the RDL → glute activation suffers (too little eccentric stretching) AND lumbar-hyperextension risk rises. 30s of pre-stretch acutely releases enough tension to enlarge ROM.
3. **Study anchors:**
   - Behm & Chaouachi 2011 — sweet spot 30s/muscle
   - Kataura 2019 — 30s vs. non-stretching: no significant difference
   - Simic 2013 meta — ≤60s per muscle = clinically irrelevant
4. **In the framework:** an athlete-specific pre-RDL static obligation (e.g. 2×30s per side, documented in `config/athlete_static.md`) is sport-scientifically well grounded. Source persisted here.

## Findings

**Pre-lift stretch dose-response** (summarised from [pre-exercise-stretching.md](pre-exercise-stretching.md)):

| Duration per muscle | Strength effect |
|---------------------|------------------|
| ≤ 30s | No significant difference; slight improvement possible with ROM limit |
| 30-45s | -1.9% (irrelevant) |
| 45-60s | -3.0% (marginal) |
| ≥ 60s | -4.6% (significant) |
| 120s+ | -5.4% (clear) |

**Hamstring-specific logic (Movement Fix, Athletes Choice Massage, FlossPoint):**
- Shortened hamstrings = active hip-flexion limitation
- RDL requires 70-90° hip flexion with an upright back
- If hamstrings "brake" before the lift → either lumbar rounds (injury risk) or ROM is incomplete (glute stimulus reduced)
- 30s stretch acutely releases neuro-mechanical tension (5-15 min effect window)

**Source status:**
> "30 seconds of static stretching per muscle group resulted in increased ROM and either no change or beneficial effects on strength and power performances." (Simic 2013)

→ 2×30s per side (standing pre-RDL) sits cleanly in the evidence-based sweet spot.

## Application in framework

- **`config/athlete_static.md`** — pre-RDL static-hamstring measure (athlete-specifically dosed) → source anchor persisted here.
- **`framework/agents/specialist-complementary.md`** should embed the pre-stretch obligation in the `intervals_icu` description when an RDL is prescribed (e.g. "WU: 2×30s hamstring static per side, then RDL").
- **Cross-reference** to [pre-exercise-stretching.md](pre-exercise-stretching.md) (N1) and [maximal-strength-protocols.md](maximal-strength-protocols.md) (M8).
- **Do not let scheduling quietly remove the RDL from a shortened-hamstring
  athlete's programme.** The RDL is one of the few movements that reaches genuine
  peak stretch, which makes it the structural stimulus for fascicle length
  (reported gains of roughly +5 to +9 % with eccentric long-length work) — a
  mobility measure alone does not produce that adaptation. The same peak stretch
  also makes it the most damage-prone option in the 24–48 h before a long
  endurance session, so it is tempting to substitute a ballistic hip hinge
  (kettlebell swing) that never reaches end-range. That swap is legitimate **for
  that slot only**: the ballistic variant is acutely safer but trains no fascicle
  length. Treat it as a *placement* decision — keep the RDL in the programme at
  ≥ 72 h from the long session — not as a substitution. Source:
  [ballistic-hip-hinge-vs-eccentric-rdl-before-longrun.md](ballistic-hip-hinge-vs-eccentric-rdl-before-longrun.md).

## Open questions / Caveats

1. **With acute hamstring injury** pre-stretching is contraindicated — do not disturb the healing phase.
2. **Dynamic stretching alternative:** for athletes without strong hamstring shortening, leg swings + hip rotations without a static element may be sufficient. Static is specifically indicated with documented shortening.
3. **Other pre-lift stretch candidates** (hip flexor before squats, pec minor before bench): the same dose-response logic applies.
