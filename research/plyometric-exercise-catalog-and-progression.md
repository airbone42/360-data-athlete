# Plyometric exercise catalog — SSC classification, tissue demand, between-exercise progression

**Erstellt:** 2026-07-05

## TL;DR

1. **Two classification axes drive exercise selection:** (a) **SSC coupling time** — slow-SSC (>250 ms ground contact, concentric-dominant, e.g. Squat Jump, Broad Jump, Box Jump) vs. fast-SSC (<250 ms, reflex/reactive, e.g. Pogo, Drop Jump, Bounding); (b) **primary tissue demand** — ankle/Achilles-dominant (Pogo, Drop Jump, Bound) vs. hip/quad/glute-dominant (Squat Jump, Broad Jump, Split-Squat Jump, Skater). Coaches pick along both axes together — spare an irritated tendon by shifting the tissue axis, spare CNS by shifting the SSC axis.
2. **Between-exercise progression ladder** (maps onto the framework Level 1→2→3 in [plyometric-progression-levels.md](plyometric-progression-levels.md)):
   Level 1 (slow-SSC, bilateral, low amplitude) → **Squat Jump → Box Jump (step-down) → Broad Jump** → Level 2 (fast-SSC bilateral + slow-SSC unilateral) → **Pogo Hops (bilateral) → Split-Squat Jump → Skater/Lateral Bound → Single-Leg Broad/Vertical Jump** → Level 3 (fast-SSC unilateral, reactive) → **Bounding (A/B/true) → Depth Jump 20–30 cm → Depth Jump 40 cm+ / Single-Leg Hopping series → Single-Leg Drop Jump**.
3. **Advance criteria between exercises** (not just volume): (i) landing quality — quiet, symmetric, no valgus drift, no compensations; (ii) for fast-SSC: **RSI stable or rising** (ratio flight-time / contact-time; ~1.5–2.0 base, 2.0–2.5 ready for intensive plyo, 2.5+ reactive-ready) with ground-contact-time not creeping upward; (iii) **symptom-free** across the same-tissue 24–48 h DOMS window ([doms-peak-timing.md](doms-peak-timing.md)); (iv) for unilateral progression: symmetric single-leg 20-hop test at VAS <2/10 ([achilles-rehab-phases.md](achilles-rehab-phases.md)); (v) for depth-jump introduction: 6–8 weeks stable Level-2 + max box height ≤ athlete's standing vertical jump.
4. **Running-economy transfer** is well-established (small-to-moderate effect on RE at ≤12 km/h; Llanos-Lagos/Ramirez-Campillo 2024 meta-analysis; Saunders 2006). The economy driver is **tendon stiffness + neuromuscular reactive stiffness**, so fast-SSC (Pogo, Bound, Drop Jump) carries the specificity — but **tendon adaptation itself responds better to long-duration strain** (Bohm/Arampatzis 2014: ~3 s at 4.5–6.5 % strain), so plyometrics *complement* heavy-slow resistance for tendon health, they do not replace it.
5. **Achilles / ankle risk map:** ankle/Achilles peak load rises sharply along the fast-SSC axis (Pogo → Bound → Drop Jump). During a tendinopathy freeze or early phase-3 return, the *concentric-dominant, slow-SSC, hip/quad-biased* exercises (Squat Jump low amplitude, Box Jump with **step-down**, Broad Jump moderate, later Split-Squat Jump) remain available and load the Achilles least. **Jump-down / drop / depth work is contraindicated** until phase-4 clearance. On an ankle with anterolateral-impingement risk or chronic instability, **lateral/inversion-loading exercises (Skater, unilateral Lateral Bound, unstable-surface hops) are the highest-risk class** — sagittal-plane plyos are safer; land with the foot flat, ankle centered, controlled dorsiflexion.

## Findings

### 1. Classification framework — the two axes coaches select along

**Axis A — SSC coupling time (ground contact):**

- **Slow-SSC (>250 ms):** concentric-dominant, force-production emphasis, low reactive demand. Suited to teaching landing mechanics, absorbing amortization, and hip/quad/glute power. Includes Squat Jump (CMJ), Box Jump (jump-up), Broad Jump / Standing Long Jump, Tuck Jump low amplitude, Split-Squat Jump.
- **Fast-SSC (<250 ms):** reflex-dominant, stretch-reflex + tendon-recoil emphasis; carries running-economy transfer via Achilles/calf stiffness. Includes Pogo Hops, Ankle Hops, true Bounding, Hurdle Hops (continuous), Drop Jump / Depth Jump, Single-Leg Hopping series.

The 250 ms threshold is the operative cut-off in the sports-science literature (Flanagan/Comyns 2008, Plyomat/Science-for-Sport reviews). Fast-SSC training only delivers its adaptation if **ground-contact-time actually stays below the threshold** — a Drop Jump landed with 400 ms GCT is training slow-SSC, not fast-SSC; RSI monitoring catches this drift.

**Axis B — Primary tissue demand:**

- **Ankle / Achilles / calf-dominant:** Pogo Hops, Ankle Hops, Bounding, Drop Jump (low box), single-leg Hopping. Peak tendon load rises steeply with drop height and unilateral loading; a 40 cm Drop Jump is one of the highest Achilles-strain interventions available in strength & conditioning.
- **Hip / glute / quad-dominant:** Squat Jump (CMJ), Broad Jump / Standing Long Jump, Box Jump (jump-up, step-down), Split-Squat Jump, Skater/Lateral Bound (also loads hip-abductor chain). Ankle load exists but is not the dominant tissue.
- **Mixed / whole-chain:** Tuck Jump, Hurdle Hops (continuous), Depth Jump with high box (progressively becomes ankle-dominant).

### 3. Between-exercise progression ladder + advance criteria

**Level 1 — foundation (slow-SSC, bilateral, low amplitude):**

Order: **Squat Jump → Box Jump (step-down) → Broad Jump → Tuck Jump low-amplitude**.

Advance to next exercise when:

- Landing is quiet, symmetric, no knee valgus, no forward torso collapse, no compensatory arm swing across ≥ 10 consecutive reps.
- Same-day + next-morning tissue check: no calf/Achilles/knee/lumbar next-day soreness beyond baseline; DOMS peak within the [doms-peak-timing.md](doms-peak-timing.md) 24–48 h window and cleared before next session.
- Consistency: ≥ 3 sessions of the current exercise at target volume without technical drift.

Advance to Level 2 when the whole Level-1 chain is checked AND a bilateral Pogo trial (2×10) shows: symmetric bounce, contact-time visually short (<250 ms), no Achilles morning soreness the next day.

**Level 2 — reactive bilateral + slow-SSC unilateral:**

Order: **Pogo Hops bilateral → Split-Squat Jump → Skater / Lateral Bound (only if ankle stable) → Single-Leg Broad/Vertical Jump (slow-SSC unilateral) → Hurdle Hops bilateral**.

Advance criteria per exercise:

- **Pogo:** GCT stays <250 ms across the set; foot lands quiet, flat, mid-foot; no cumulative arch/heel fatigue. Progress from bilateral in-place → bilateral travelling → unilateral hopping in place → unilateral travelling.
- **Split-Squat Jump / Single-Leg jumps:** left-right symmetry within ~10 % on jump distance/height; front knee tracks over foot; no lumbar hyperextension.
- **Skater / Lateral Bound:** land with ankle centered, hip stacked; no ankle-roll episodes across the set; hip-abductor endurance holds through the last rep. **Gate for ankles with anterolateral-impingement risk or chronic lateral instability — see Section 5.**
- **Hurdle Hops:** the moment contact-time drifts long (>250 ms visually / RSI drops), cap the set. Not a "grind through" exercise.

Advance to Level 3 when: symmetric single-leg 20-hop test tolerated at VAS <2/10 for the target tissue (Silbernagel 2020 return-to-plyo criterion); ≥ 6–8 weeks stable Level-2 work per [plyometric-progression-levels.md](plyometric-progression-levels.md); RSI on a low Drop Jump (20 cm) ≥ ~1.5.

**Level 3 — reactive unilateral / shock:**

Order: **Bounding (A → B → true bounds) → Depth Jump 20–30 cm → Depth Jump 40 cm+ → continuous Single-Leg Hopping series → Single-Leg Drop Jump**.

Advance criteria:

- **RSI-anchored:** progress the depth-jump box height only if RSI on the *current* box is stable or rising (~2.0 solid base, ~2.5 ready for intensive work) AND ground-contact-time is not creeping upward. If GCT rises, drop back a step — the athlete is training slow-SSC dressed up as fast-SSC.
- **Box height ≤ standing vertical jump.** The classic literature ceiling; a box higher than the athlete's vertical converts propulsive shock into deceleration damage.
- **Symptom-free through the DOMS window** on the same tissue for two consecutive sessions before adding intensity.

**Volume anchor** (contacts/session) stays governed by [plyometric-progression-levels.md](plyometric-progression-levels.md). This document adds the *which exercise* dimension; volume is the *how much* dimension. Both compose.

| Hip-extension power without ground impact | Kettlebell Swing (two-handed) | Broad Jump *(adds ground impact)*, Trap-Bar jump |
| **Knee-compression-sparing** (patellofemoral symptoms under load) | Kettlebell Swing, Broad Jump, hinge-pattern work | Trap-Bar Deadlift; **avoid** deep squat patterns and Split-Squat Jump until quiet |
| **Achilles-sparing when tendon irritated** | Squat Jump, Broad Jump, Box Jump (step-down), Split-Squat Jump (moderate), Kettlebell Swing | Concentric-dominant, slow-SSC, non-drop |
| **Ankle-lateral-instability-sparing** | Sagittal-plane bilateral (Squat Jump, Pogo bilateral, Broad Jump); **avoid** Skater, unilateral Lateral Bound, unstable-surface hops | Slow-SSC bilateral only until instability resolved |

### 5. Endurance-running integration

**Running-economy transfer:** meta-analyses converge that 6–14 weeks of plyometric training, 2×/week, improves running economy in middle- and long-distance runners with small-to-moderate effect (Saunders et al. 2006; Ramirez-Campillo et al. reviews; Llanos-Lagos, Ramirez-Campillo, Moran, Sáez de Villarreal 2024). Effect is clearest at moderate speeds (≤12 km/h) and driven by (a) tendon stiffness gain, (b) leg-spring stiffness / reactive strength, (c) neuromuscular co-activation improvement — not by VO2max change.

**Tendon-adaptation nuance (Bohm/Arampatzis 2014):** the Achilles adapts optimally to **long-duration strain** (~3 s at 4.5–6.5 % strain) — plyometrics with sub-second contact times are *not* the optimal tendon-adaptation stimulus. Practically: heavy-slow resistance / HSR calf work (~3 s eccentric-concentric) builds tendon capacity; plyometrics train the *use* of that capacity as leg-spring stiffness. Both belong in a runner's plan; they are complements, not substitutes.

**Weekly integration around endurance quality:**

- Same-day sequencing: run quality **before** plyo, or ≥ 6 h gap ([concurrent-training-interference.md](concurrent-training-interference.md)).
- Cross-day: honour the 24–48 h DOMS window between eccentric leg work and leg-driven endurance quality ([doms-peak-timing.md](doms-peak-timing.md); Leg-quality cross-modality DOMS spacing rule in framework CLAUDE.md).
- Pre-Z4/Z5 same-day / prior-24 h: eccentric-loaded calf work (heavy calf raise, Bounds, Depth Jumps) is **PAP-inhibitory, not facilitatory** ([eccentric-calf-pap-inhibition.md](eccentric-calf-pap-inhibition.md)) — Pogo Hops as strides-adjacent priming (short contacts, no eccentric load spike) is compatible; Bounds/Depth Jumps are not.

### 6. Ninja / obstacle athletics integration

Obstacle-course events (warped wall, gap jumps, lache launches, dynamic transitions) share the plyometric qualities but weight them differently:

- **Fast-SSC vertical + horizontal power** (Depth Jump, Bounding, Broad Jump, single-leg hopping) directly transfers to warped-wall pop, gap crossings, and dynamic launches from unstable surfaces.
- **Frontal-plane power + landing stability** (Skater / Lateral Bound, single-leg controlled landing) transfers to lateral transitions and asymmetric obstacle landings.
- **Upper-lower coordination** (kettlebell swing, medicine-ball throw, kip-swing plyo) is under-covered by pure lower-limb plyo — an obstacle athlete needs upper-body / trunk explosive work in addition.

For a runner-plus-ninja athlete, the *shared* driver is Achilles/calf reactive stiffness (Pogo, Bound) — both disciplines benefit from the same base. The **ninja-specific add-on** is the frontal-plane / lateral / single-leg dynamic work, but *only once ankle stability supports it* (Section 5 caveat).

### 7. Achilles-tendinopathy return-to-plyo + ankle anterolateral considerations

**Achilles tendinopathy freeze / early Silbernagel phase 3:** stay in the **concentric-dominant, slow-SSC, non-drop** class — Squat Jump, Box Jump with **step-down** (never jump-down), Broad Jump moderate amplitude, Split-Squat Jump once bilateral is tolerated. **Contraindicated** until phase-4 clearance ([achilles-rehab-phases.md](achilles-rehab-phases.md)): all Drop/Depth Jumps, unilateral hopping series, Bounding at true amplitude, unilateral hurdle hops. Pogo Hops sit at the entry to reactive tendon load — introduce only after: 6–8 weeks HSR with progressive load, bilateral heel raise at VAS <3/10 with added weight, and 20 single-leg hops at VAS <2/10. Load progression is **height/amplitude before unilateral before shock**, never in another order.

**Ankle with anterolateral-impingement risk / chronic lateral instability:** the *inversion-loading* exercises are the highest-risk class — anterolateral impingement is triggered by exactly the inversion moment that occurs on a laterally-displaced landing (Physiopedia; ScienceDirect state-of-the-art review). Rules:

- **Prefer sagittal-plane, bilateral, flat-foot landings.** Pogo bilateral, Squat Jump, Broad Jump = safe. Skater / Lateral Bound / unilateral lateral hop = high-risk.
- **Land with the foot flat, ankle centered, controlled dorsiflexion.** Toe-only landings drive the talus forward against the anterior capsule (impingement mechanism).
- **Avoid end-range dorsiflexion under load.** Depth jumps from height and deep-landing Broad Jumps push dorsiflexion into the impingement range — cap amplitude.
- **Unstable-surface plyo (BOSU, foam pad)** amplifies inversion-moment exposure — off-menu until stability normalises.

## Open questions / Caveats

1. **RSI measurement in a home-gym context** is only feasible with a jump mat / accelerometer app. Without instrumentation, coaches proxy via GCT (visual: "does the contact still look explosive after rep 6?") and landing quality. A cheap RSI-substitute for home use is an open item.
2. **Tendon-adaptation vs. running-economy training are partly orthogonal** (Bohm/Arampatzis). Plyometrics build reactive stiffness; HSR builds tendon capacity. The optimal weekly stack for a runner with a tendinopathy history mixes both — the split ratio is not yet well studied.
3. **Unilateral-Lateral-Bound / Skater in the presence of resolved lateral-ankle instability**: how much stability is enough? Return-to-lateral-plyo criteria (single-leg balance duration, hop test, Cumberland Ankle Instability Tool score) are under-standardised; head-coach + physio-consultant judgement for now.
4. **Depth-jump box-height progression rate** varies across literature (20 → 30 → 40 → 60 cm typical; some recommend RSI-only, some fixed height). The framework leans on RSI-anchored progression + box ≤ standing-vertical ceiling.
5. **Ninja-specific plyo transfer** is coach-source-heavy, not peer-review-heavy — the specific transfer of e.g. Depth Jump to warped-wall pop is inferred from general reactive-strength → jump-height evidence, not directly tested.
